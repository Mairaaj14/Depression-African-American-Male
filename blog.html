Research About
Depression in Men in the
African American Community.


Depression is one of the most common mental disorders that we might all get to experience through out our lifetime. One of the main symptoms of this mental disease is pretty much disconnecting from the real world slowly. Not being interested in anything around us anymore, feeling like you are in a black hole literally, nothing matters anymore. This is a feeling that is definitely scary to experience, and it is proven by facts that African American men are more likely to suffer from this than any other race. It was of my interest to deep more into the factors of why this is happening. I currently live in a neighborhood where most of my neighbors are African Americans. I wanted to dig deep into the facts and find out why they are one of the most affected communities. There's many contributors that lead to depression, the one that most stood out to me were how minorities, in this case African Americans are treated just because of their background.

Discrimination and stereotypes takes a big role into leading a person to depression. "...there is a growing body of evidence demosntrating the role of factors such as socioeconomic status, social stressors(including discrimination), and psychological coping resources for depressive symptoms among African American men." says Dr.Mathew D. Gayman. Dr. Gayman and collegues from Georgia State University conducted a community based sample to identify the risk and protective factors for mental health among adult black men. Other factors such as family support, mastery, self-esteem, chronic stressors, and the biggest one - discrimination were found to be independently associated with depression. African Americans are more likely to be victims of police brutality and racial discrimination than any other race. Slavery is also a factor that has an affect in African American's mental health. Growing up for most was hard also because of the macroeconomics that surrounded them. Many live in a low income meighborhood surrounded by drugs, gang members and other cycles that make it harder for them to get out of the low income cycle. This leads to poor health and poor life decisions that also lead to depression. Although depression is one of the most common mental disorders, the lack of resources is a big one in the African American community.

According to a video by Dr.Harold, a professor from Michigan State University, black men are the less likely to seek help when they are experiencing depression. It is interesting and reveling to find out exaclty why they don't seek help. Maybe the stigma of growing up without needing to ask for help is a big contributor. Many minorities are not offered the resources and help other groups might get and this leads to something that is not acceptable or known as "normal". Not making these resources accessible is something that needs to change in low income communitites. I believe mental health should be taught early in schools, because it contributes at how we see the world around us and also helps us take control of our feelings. I also believe mental health awareness should be normalized and practiced with younger generations. So they learn to seek help when needed, and be able to distinguish bad emotions and cope with them.
